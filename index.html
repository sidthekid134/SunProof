Clear answers

Sun safety,
without the myths.

Practical guidance for the questions that usually make sun protection feel confusing.

Do I need sunscreen on cloudy days?+

Often, yes. UV radiation can still reach your skin through cloud cover. Check the UV index rather than judging by temperature or brightness alone.

What does “broad spectrum” mean?+

It means the product is designed to protect against both UVA and UVB radiation. Look for “broad spectrum” on the label.

Is a higher SPF always better?+

SPF 30 is a strong everyday baseline. Higher SPF can add margin, but correct application and reapplication matter more than relying on a very high number.

Does darker skin still need protection?+

Yes. Melanin provides some natural protection, but people of every skin tone can experience UV damage and should use sun-safe habits.
